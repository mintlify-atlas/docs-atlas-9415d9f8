Services represent active instances of products that customers have purchased. When a customer orders a product, Paymenter creates a service record that tracks the lifecycle, billing, and configuration of that purchase.

## Service Statuses

Services progress through different statuses during their lifecycle:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Pending" icon="clock">
    Service has been ordered but not yet activated. This occurs when:
    - Initial invoice is unpaid
    - Server provisioning is in progress
    - Manual approval is required
    
    Pending services do not generate renewal invoices.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Active" icon="circle-check">
    Service is fully operational and accessible to the customer. Active services:
    - Generate automatic renewal invoices
    - Can be upgraded or cancelled
    - Show server extension management options
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Suspended" icon="pause">
    Service has been temporarily disabled, usually due to:
    - Unpaid invoices
    - Terms of service violation
    - Customer request
    
    Suspended services can be reactivated by paying outstanding invoices or admin action.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Cancelled" icon="circle-xmark">
    Service has been terminated and is no longer active. Cancelled services:
    - No longer generate invoices
    - May be deleted after a retention period
    - Cannot be reactivated (customer must order again)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Customer Service Management

Customers access their services through the client portal at `/services`.

### Viewing Service Details

The service detail page displays:

- **Service Information**: Product name, custom label, status, and expiration date
- **Pricing**: Current price and billing cycle
- **Configuration**: Selected config options and their values
- **Extension Views**: Custom management interfaces from server extensions
- **Recent Invoices**: Payment history for this service

### Custom Service Labels

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Service">
    Customer clicks on a service from their services list
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Edit Label">
    Click the edit icon next to the service name
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Custom Label">
    Provide a memorable name like "Production Website" or "Development VPS"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save">
    The custom label appears throughout the portal instead of the default "Product Name #123"
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Encourage customers to label their services, especially if they have multiple services of the same product. It makes invoice identification much easier.
</Tip>

## Extension Actions

When a product is connected to a server extension, the service page shows management options:

### Action Types

1. **Buttons**: Trigger server actions (restart, reinstall, backup)
2. **Views**: Display custom interfaces (console, file manager, statistics)
3. **Fields**: Show information (IP address, username, password)

<Note>
Extension actions only appear for active services. Pending or suspended services may have limited functionality.
</Note>

### Example Actions

Depending on the server extension:

- **Pterodactyl**: Game server console, file manager, reinstall
- **Virtualizor**: VNC console, reboot, rebuild
- **cPanel**: Control panel login, create backups

## Service Upgrades

Customers can upgrade their services to higher-tier products or modify configuration options.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check Upgradability">
    Service must be active and have available upgrade paths defined
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select New Product">
    Choose from configured upgrade products with matching billing cycles
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Modify Config Options">
    Change upgradable configuration options (e.g., increase disk space)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Prorated Cost">
    System calculates the difference based on remaining service time
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Pay Upgrade Invoice">
    If there's a cost difference, an invoice is generated
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Service Updated">
    Upon payment, the service is upgraded and new configuration is applied
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Upgrade Pricing

Paymenter uses prorated billing for upgrades:

- **Credit for Unused Time**: Calculates remaining value of current service
- **Charge for New Service**: Prorates the new service price for the remaining period
- **Net Difference**: Customer pays (or receives credit for) the difference

<Info>
If an upgrade results in a downgrade with a lower price, the customer receives account credits (if enabled in settings).
</Info>

## Service Cancellations

Customers can request service cancellation from the service detail page.

### Cancellation Types

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Immediate Cancellation">
    Service is terminated immediately and customer loses access. No refund is provided for remaining time.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="End of Billing Period">
    Service remains active until the current billing period expires. This is the recommended option for customers.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Cancellation Process

1. Customer clicks "Cancel Service" button
2. Selects cancellation type and provides reason
3. Cancellation request is created with status "pending"
4. Admin reviews and approves the cancellation
5. Service status changes to "cancelled" at the appropriate time

<Warning>
One-time and free services cannot be cancelled through the standard process. Contact the admin for termination.
</Warning>

## Billing Agreements

Customers can attach saved payment methods to recurring services for automatic billing.

### Setting Up Auto-Pay

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Service Details">
    Navigate to the service you want to set up auto-pay for
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Billing Agreement">
    Find the billing agreement section
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Payment Method">
    Choose from saved payment methods compatible with the service currency
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm">
    The selected payment method will be charged automatically when invoices are generated
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Billing agreements can also be set during invoice payment, and applied to all recurring services on that invoice.
</Tip>

## Admin Service Management

Administrators have additional service management capabilities.

### Admin Panel Features

- **Create Manual Services**: Add services without going through checkout
- **Change Status**: Override service status (activate, suspend, cancel)
- **Adjust Pricing**: Modify the service price without affecting the product
- **Edit Configuration**: Change config options after creation
- **Extend Expiration**: Give customers more time
- **View Service History**: See all changes via audit log

### Managing Customer Services

1. Navigate to **Administration** → **Services**
2. Find the service using search or filters
3. Click on service to view details
4. Use the edit form to modify service parameters

<Note>
Changes to service pricing only affect that specific service. The product's pricing remains unchanged for new orders.
</Note>

### Bulk Actions

Perform actions on multiple services:

- Suspend multiple services (e.g., for unpaid invoices)
- Cancel services in bulk
- Export service data for reporting

## Automated Service Workflows

Paymenter automates several service-related tasks:

### Invoice Generation

- Recurring services generate invoices automatically before expiration
- Invoice due date is configurable (default: 7 days before expiration)
- Services with billing agreements are charged automatically

### Status Changes

- Services become **Active** when initial invoice is paid
- Services are **Suspended** when renewal invoices remain unpaid past due date
- Services are **Cancelled** when cancellation requests are approved

### Extension Provisioning

- Server extensions automatically provision new services
- Suspension triggers extension suspend action
- Cancellation triggers extension terminate action

## Service Reports

Track service metrics for business insights:

- **Active Services by Product**: See which products are most popular
- **Churn Rate**: Monitor cancellations over time
- **Revenue by Service**: Calculate MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
- **Expiring Services**: Identify services needing renewal attention

## Troubleshooting

### Service Stuck in Pending

- Check if initial invoice is paid
- Review server extension logs for provisioning errors
- Manually activate the service from admin panel if needed

### Extension Actions Not Showing

- Verify service status is "active"
- Check that product has a server assigned
- Ensure extension is enabled and properly configured

### Auto-Billing Not Working

- Confirm billing agreement is attached to service
- Verify payment gateway supports automatic charging
- Check that customer's payment method hasn't expired
