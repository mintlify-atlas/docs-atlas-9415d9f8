## Invoice Statuses

Invoices progress through different statuses as they're processed:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Pending" icon="clock">
    Invoice has been issued but not yet paid. Customers can:
    - View invoice details
    - Pay via any configured payment gateway
    - Download PDF copy
    
    Pending invoices have a due date (typically 7 days from issue).
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Paid" icon="circle-check">
    Invoice has been paid in full. The system automatically:
    - Activates or renews associated services
    - Sends payment confirmation email
    - Updates customer credit balance if overpaid
    
    Paid invoices cannot be modified.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Cancelled" icon="ban">
    Invoice has been voided by an administrator. Common reasons:
    - Customer cancelled order before payment
    - Billing error requiring new invoice
    - Service no longer needed
    
    Cancelled invoices are retained for record-keeping.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Invoice Components

Each invoice contains:

### Header Information

- **Invoice Number**: Unique identifier (can be customized format)
- **Issue Date**: When the invoice was created
- **Due Date**: Payment deadline
- **Bill To**: Customer name and properties (address, tax ID, etc.)
- **Company Details**: Your business information

### Invoice Items

Line items representing the charges:

- **Description**: What is being billed (includes date range for services)
- **Quantity**: Number of units
- **Unit Price**: Price per unit
- **Total**: Quantity × Unit Price

### Totals

- **Subtotal**: Sum of all line items before tax
- **Tax**: Calculated based on customer location and tax rates
- **Total**: Final amount due
- **Paid**: Amount already received
- **Remaining**: Outstanding balance

## Automatic Invoice Generation

Paymenter generates invoices automatically for recurring services.

### How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Cron Job Runs">
    Daily cron job checks for services expiring soon
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Invoice Created">
    For each expiring service, an invoice is created with:
    - Service renewal for the next billing period
    - Any applicable taxes
    - Customer's current currency
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Email Sent">
    Customer receives email notification with:
    - Invoice details and amount
    - Payment link
    - Due date reminder
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Auto-Charge (Optional)">
    If service has a billing agreement attached:
    - Payment gateway automatically charges the saved method
    - Service renews immediately upon successful payment
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
Invoices are typically generated 7 days before service expiration. This gives customers time to review and pay before service interruption.
</Info>

## Manual Invoice Creation

Administrators can create manual invoices for various scenarios:

### Admin Panel Process

1. Navigate to **Administration** → **Invoices**
2. Click **Create Invoice**
3. Select customer
4. Choose currency
5. Set due date
6. Add invoice items:
   - Description
   - Price
   - Quantity
   - Optional: Link to a service
7. Review and save

<Tip>
Manual invoices are useful for one-time charges like setup fees, custom development, or account adjustments.
</Tip>

## Customer Payment Process

Customers can pay invoices through multiple methods.

### Standard Payment Flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Invoice">
    Customer receives email notification or views invoice in portal
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Pay">
    Invoice page shows the amount due and available payment methods
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Payment Method">
    Choose from:
    - Payment gateways (credit card, PayPal, etc.)
    - Account credits
    - Saved payment methods (billing agreements)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete Payment">
    Redirected to payment gateway or processed directly
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirmation">
    Payment status updates automatically
    - Success: Invoice marked paid, service activated
    - Failed: Error message shown, can retry
    - Processing: Status page with automatic refresh
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Payment Options

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Payment Gateways">
    Credit cards, PayPal, bank transfers, and other external payment methods. Gateways are added via extensions.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Account Credits">
    Use existing credit balance to pay invoices. If credits are insufficient, can pay remaining balance via another method.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Saved Payment Methods">
    Billing agreements allow one-click payment with previously saved cards or accounts. Can also set as default for automatic billing.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Partial Payments

Customers can make partial payments using credits:

1. Select "Account Credit" as payment method
2. System applies available credits to invoice
3. If insufficient, remaining balance shown
4. Select another payment method for the rest

<Note>
Full payment is required to activate services. Partially paid invoices remain in "pending" status.
</Note>

## Invoice Snapshots

To maintain accurate historical records, Paymenter can "snapshot" customer information when invoices are created.

### What Gets Captured

- Customer name at time of invoice
- Address and contact properties
- Tax rate and tax country
- Company details and bill-to text

### Why It Matters

Snapshots ensure that:

- Invoices reflect customer information at time of purchase
- Historical invoices remain accurate even if customer updates their details
- Tax calculations are preserved for auditing
- PDF invoices always show correct information

<Info>
Invoice snapshots can be enabled/disabled in settings. When disabled, invoices always show current customer information.
</Info>

## Invoice Transactions

Each payment attempt creates a transaction record:

### Transaction Statuses

- **Processing**: Payment is being verified by gateway
- **Succeeded**: Payment completed successfully
- **Failed**: Payment was declined or encountered error

### Transaction Details

- Amount paid
- Payment gateway used
- Transaction ID from gateway
- Timestamp
- Status and error messages

<Tip>
Multiple transactions can exist for a single invoice (e.g., failed attempt followed by successful payment). The invoice total tracks remaining balance.
</Tip>

## Tax Handling

Paymenter supports flexible tax calculation:

### Tax Configuration

1. Create tax rates in **Administration** → **Tax Rates**
2. Specify:
   - Tax name (VAT, GST, Sales Tax)
   - Rate percentage
   - Countries where it applies
3. Tax is automatically calculated based on customer's country

### Inclusive vs. Exclusive Tax

Configure whether prices include or exclude tax:

- **Exclusive**: Tax added to price at checkout
- **Inclusive**: Tax already included in displayed price

<Warning>
Changing tax settings affects new invoices only. Existing invoices retain their original tax calculations.
</Warning>

## Recurring Services & Invoices

Understanding the relationship between services and invoices:

### Initial Order

1. Customer orders a product with recurring plan
2. Order creates a service in "pending" status
3. Invoice is generated for first billing period (+ setup fee if applicable)
4. Customer pays invoice
5. Service status changes to "active"
6. Service expiration date is set

### Renewals

1. Cron job detects service expiring soon
2. New invoice generated for next billing period
3. If billing agreement exists, automatic charge attempted
4. Upon payment, service expiration extended
5. Service remains active

### Failed Renewals

1. Invoice remains unpaid past due date
2. Service is suspended (optional grace period)
3. Customer cannot access service
4. Reminder emails sent
5. If paid within retention period, service reactivated
6. If unpaid too long, service may be cancelled

<Note>
Grace periods and suspension timing are configurable in settings.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting

### Invoice Not Generated

- Verify cron job is running (check last run time)
- Ensure service has recurring plan (not one-time or free)
- Check service status is "active"
- Confirm service expiration date is within invoice generation window

### Payment Processing Issues

- Check payment gateway is enabled and configured
- Review gateway transaction logs for errors
- Verify currency is supported by selected gateway
- Test gateway connection from admin panel

### Tax Not Calculating

- Confirm tax rate exists for customer's country
- Check customer has country property set
- Verify tax rate is enabled
- Review tax calculation settings
